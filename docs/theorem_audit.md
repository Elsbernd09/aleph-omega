# Theorem Audit Records

## Purpose

Phase 16B adds theorem audit records to Project Aleph-Omega.

The audit system checks whether formal claims are recorded responsibly.

It does not prove the claims.

Instead, it checks whether claims have:

- clear statements
- explicit scope
- verification levels
- evidence records
- limitations
- conjectural marking when appropriate

---

## Audit Statuses

Each claim receives one of three audit statuses:

- passed
- warning
- failed

A passed audit means the claim is responsibly recorded.

A warning means the claim may still be usable but needs caution.

A failed audit means the claim record is malformed or misleading.

---

## Why This Matters

A serious mathematical project should not only contain theorem-like statements.

It should also audit those statements.

The audit layer helps prevent overclaiming by checking whether each claim says:

- what it actually proves or tests
- what evidence supports it
- where its scope ends
- what remains conjectural
